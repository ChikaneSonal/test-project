Treatment Overview

Total Knee Replacement is a surgery to remove and replace the entire damaged knee joint with an artificial joint.

The procedure aims to relieve pain, improve mobility, and restore function to the knee joint.

During the surgery, the damaged portions of the knee joint are removed and replaced with

metal and plastic components designed to replicate the movement of a healthy knee.

Recovery from Total Knee Replacement involves physical therapy and gradual return to normal activities.

Most patients experience significant improvement in pain and mobility within a few months post-surgery.

Eligibility For Treatment

Any individual who has tried diet and exercise but failed to lose weight is considered as a good candidate for gastric bypass.
Although gastric bypass aids in weight loss, it is not an appropriate option for everyone.
Based on the overall health and certain medical guidelines, the doctor will decide whether you are eligible for gastric bypass or not.

In general, gastric bypass could be a good option if:

  • The BMI (body mass index) is 40 or more.
  • Your BMI is 30 or more with severe weight-related health conditions like type-2 diabetes and high blood pressure.
  • You are willing to make permanent changes in lifestyle.
